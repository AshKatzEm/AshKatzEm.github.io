Classroom data experiment

In the very beggining of 9/23, I was asked to teach high school algebra to students with learning disabilities through 6/24. I'm obsessed with trying to improve education. This was a silver platter offer to try out some of my ideas, many of which invovled emerging educational technologies in one form or another. It was an incredible experience and it both validated some of my ideas and opened my eyes to new ones. I think I learned more than the students and they definitly learned some stuff. Anyway, I designed a two year high school algebra 1 curriculum from the ground up and at the core, I used an educational platform called Quizizz. The goal was to minimize lecture as much as possible and instead learn through classwide interactive problem solving. A massive ancillary benefit of this system was that it generated a steady stream of data. I documented my attemps to do something with it on LinkedIn.
